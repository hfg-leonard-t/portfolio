VIRTUAL REALITY Memorise
scroll down
Semester Summer Semester 2022
Group Members Marie Winterbauer, Marvin Ebert
Personal contribution 3D Modeling, Conceptualization, Video rendering, Digital prototyping
Description Our project focuses on making memory techniques, used by world memory champions, accessible and fun through a Virtual Reality (VR) learning concept. We have developed a VR prototype that utilizes the Loci method, a powerful mnemonic device, to help users memorize complex information efficiently.

The Loci method involves associating items to be remembered with specific locations within a virtual space. In our VR environment, for example, users learn to associate the Latin name for the thigh bone, "Femur," with a visually engaging scene involving a lemur (sounding nearly the same as "Femur") sitting on the thigh bone of a women. Such vivid, unusual associations make it easier to recall information.

Our research identified two main challenges in using mnemonic methods: the learning curve and time investment required. To address these, we experimented with various prototypes, from simple associations to a 3D environment created in Blender, and received guidance from two-time memory world champion Johannes Mallow. This iterative process led to a user-friendly VR application that provides a distraction-free and immersive learning experience.

This tool is especially beneficial for students and professionals in fields requiring extensive memorization, such as medicine and pharmacology. It not only saves time but also makes learning more engaging and memorable.
